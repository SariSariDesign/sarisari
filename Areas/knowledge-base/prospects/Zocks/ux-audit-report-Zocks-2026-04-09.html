Evaluation Lens

Zocks sells into financial advisors under FINRA/SEC regulatory pressure who can't use generic AI tools. The site's job is to make "privacy-first, compliant AI" the obvious answer vs. Otter and Fireflies — which advisors already know they can't use on client calls.

Why This Matters

Financial advisors lose 10-15 hours/week to administrative work. The TAM for a product that credibly removes that load is enormous — but only if the site clears the compliance bar in the first 10 seconds. Advisors who feel any ambiguity about FINRA/SEC fit default to "no."

Top 5 Findings

Ranked by estimated impact on pipeline conversion.

1
Content Hierarchy & CopywritingMajor

The compliance wedge — your strongest differentiator — isn't in the hero

The nav exposes "Privacy & Security" and "Compliance" as platform surfaces, strongly suggesting Zocks has a real story here. But the hero copy — "The AI Assistant for financial advisors" / "Get 10+ hours a week back..." — doesn't mention compliance, privacy, or FINRA/SEC fit at all. The wedge against Otter and Fireflies is invisible above the fold.

Why this matters for Zocks: The #1 reason an advisor chooses Zocks over general meeting AI is that Zocks is built to be compliant in an audited environment. Advisors already know they can't use Otter on client calls — they're on your site looking for permission to believe they've found an alternative. Make that permission the second thing they read.

CRO-05 Value Proposition Clarity / COPY-08 Proof & Credibility

Recommendation

Add a compliance ribbon directly below the hero: "Built for financial advisors — SOC 2 Type II, FINRA-compliant recording workflows, no client data used in model training." Make compliance the second thing every visitor reads — not a nav dropdown you have to go find.

Effort: LowImpact: High
2
Conversion Rate OptimizationMajor

The free trial exists — but isn't the hero CTA for self-serve advisors

Zocks offers a 14-day free trial (mentioned in the nav), but the hero CTAs are "Book a demo" and "See how it works." The trial is not promoted above the fold where solo and small-practice advisors can see it.

Why this matters for Zocks: Solo advisors — the largest segment of the wealth-management market — overwhelmingly prefer self-serve trial over booking a sales call. Burying the trial behind a nav link means the PLG path is invisible to the exact segment that prefers it, and forces those advisors into a sales motion they'll likely abandon.

CRO-04 Engagement Path Variety / CRO-01 Primary CTA Prominence

Recommendation

Add "Start 14-day free trial" as a primary or co-primary hero CTA. Route enterprise buyers to "Book a demo" via a secondary path. If possible, detect firm size and auto-weight the CTAs accordingly — solo advisor IP ranges see Trial first, enterprise sees Demo first.

Effort: LowImpact: High
3
Trust SignalsMajor

SOC 2 status not visible in the homepage or footer

The homepage mentions "Privacy and Security" as a platform section, but there is no visible SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, or FINRA-alignment badge in the hero or footer. For an advisor evaluating Zocks — or a compliance officer at Commonwealth or Osaic — this is the first filter they apply.

Why this matters for Zocks: Enterprise broker-dealers like Osaic, Commonwealth, and Cambridge would not use Zocks without SOC 2. The badge almost certainly exists — but if a visiting prospect can't see it, it doesn't help the deal. Meanwhile, every day it's invisible, a compliance officer is telling her team "I don't know if they're SOC 2 — use something else."

CRO-06 Trust Signal Placement

Recommendation

Add a security badge strip to the footer and build a dedicated "/security" trust center page with SOC 2 Type II, data-at-rest encryption, model-training opt-out, FINRA-aligned audit trail, and subprocessor list. Link from the compliance nav item.

Effort: LowImpact: High
4
Content Hierarchy & CopywritingMajor

The "10+ hours a week" stat is buried in the subhead — promote it

The headline "The AI Assistant for financial advisors" is category-clear but generic. The real wedge is in the subhead: "Get 10+ hours a week back to focus on your clients." That's the quantified outcome an advisor actually buys — and it's currently doing all the work from a supporting position.

Why this matters for Zocks: Specific, quantified outcome claims convert. "10+ hours a week" is the kind of number an advisor forwards to a colleague. Leaving it as supporting copy means the strongest asset in your pitch is doing less work than it could.

COPY-03 Headline Specificity / COPY-02 Benefit-led vs Feature-led

Recommendation

Promote the stat into the hero. Direction: "Give financial advisors 10+ hours a week back. Built for FINRA and SEC compliance." Lead with the outcome AND the compliance wedge in a single line.

Effort: LowImpact: High
5
Information ArchitectureMinor

30+ nav items creates choice paralysis

Navigation contains Platform (9 items), Features (8 items), Resources (6 items), plus Pricing, Customers, Help Center, Login, and Start Free Trial. That's 30+ discoverable links in the top chrome.

Why this matters for Zocks: Deep nav signals "we serve every possible use case" but creates choice paralysis for the very real solo advisor who just wants to know "can I use this on my client calls tomorrow?" Pruning the nav forces clearer positioning and lowers the cognitive cost of evaluation.

IA-01 Navigation Clarity

Recommendation

Consolidate. Collapse "Features" under "Platform" and prune rarely-visited items (Zocks MCP, Customization) to a deeper sub-page. Target 4-5 top-level nav items. A tighter nav makes the compliance and ROI pages easier to find — not harder.

Effort: MediumImpact: Medium
