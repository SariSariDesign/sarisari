Evaluation Lens

Vivi competes in the Australian neobank category where Up Bank has dominant mindshare and Revolut has brand gravity. The site's job is to give a skeptical Australian consumer a reason to download a new banking app — a category with extremely low download intent outside targeted campaigns.

Why This Matters

In consumer fintech, the homepage is the most expensive real estate the company owns. In Australia specifically, consumers are trust-skeptical of new banks after the 2020-2023 neobank shakeout (Xinja, Volt). Missing trust signals cost more here than in other markets.

Top 5 Findings

Ranked by estimated impact on install conversion.

1
Trust SignalsCritical

No visible ADI status or deposit protection disclosure above the fold

The homepage surfaces "ACN 653 436 689" and a Mutual Ltd. partnership in the footer. There is no above-the-fold disclosure of AFSL, ADI status, or whether deposits are covered by the Australian Financial Claims Scheme (FCS / government guarantee up to $250K).

Why this matters for Vivi: In Australia post-Xinja/Volt collapse, consumers check for ADI status before they install. The category has been burned, and the people most likely to park meaningful deposits are also the most likely to disqualify a bank that doesn't surface this clearly. Not answering this question above the fold is a silent bounce driver.

CRO-06 Trust Signal Placement / COPY-08 Proof & Credibility

Recommendation

If Vivi is an ADI (directly or via a sponsor bank), display it prominently: "Your deposits are protected up to $250,000 under the Australian Government Financial Claims Scheme." If not, transparently explain how funds are protected (trust account structure, EMI license, sponsor bank relationship). Ambiguity is the enemy here.

Effort: LowImpact: High
2
Content Hierarchy & CopywritingMajor

The best claim you have — "up to 8% p.a. at call" — isn't in the hero

Mid-page, Vivi mentions savings rates of "up to 6%" and "6-8% p.a. at call." Against the big-4 Australian banks (who pay 0.05%-4.5% on savings), that is genuinely best-in-market. But it's buried. The hero leads instead with "The AI money genius that fits in your pocket."

Why this matters for Vivi: Interest rate is the #1 reason Australians switch savings accounts. If your rate is the best in market, the rate IS the hero. "AI money genius" is a distant, soft second — a vibe, not a reason to install.

COPY-04 Information Hierarchy / CRO-05 Value Proposition Clarity

Recommendation

Hero direction: "Earn up to 8% p.a. on your savings. No minimum balance. Visa card included." Lead with the number. The AI story comes second as the "how it gets even better."

Effort: LowImpact: High
3
Conversion Rate OptimizationMajor

"Get Vivi Now" buttons with no App Store or Google Play badges

The primary CTA is "Get Vivi Now" (2 instances above fold), but the page does not display Apple App Store or Google Play badges — the standard visual shorthand for "install this app." A mobile user landing from an Instagram or TikTok ad has to guess what the button does and take an extra step.

Why this matters for Vivi: Mobile install conversion is brutally sensitive to friction. Every additional decision step between ad click and app install drops conversion by double-digit percentages. The standard App Store / Play Store badge is a visual convention users have been trained to trust and tap instantly.

CRO-02 CTA Specificity / CRO-01 Primary CTA Prominence

Recommendation

Replace or supplement "Get Vivi Now" with standard Apple/Google store badges directly in the hero. Use smart link detection to route iOS users to App Store and Android users to Play Store. Desktop users should see a QR code that opens the relevant store on their phone.

Effort: LowImpact: High
4
Trust SignalsMajor

Zero social proof — no download count, rating, testimonial, or press

The homepage shows no app store ratings, install count, star rating, customer testimonial, or press logo. The only trust signals are legal disclosures in the footer.

Why this matters for Vivi: Consumer fintech conversion is driven by "do people like me already trust this?" Without a single social proof element, every visitor is effectively the first user — a cognitive burden they don't want to carry when deciding where to park their paycheck.

CRO-07 Social Proof Depth

Recommendation

Add above-the-fold social proof: app store rating + review count, press logos if you've been covered, or "Join [X] Australians already using Vivi" if the install number is meaningful. If the product is genuinely early and the numbers are small, substitute with 3-4 testimonial quotes from real users, named, with city and first name.

Effort: LowImpact: High
5
Content Hierarchy & CopywritingMajor

"AI money genius" is a claim; the subhead doesn't back it up

Headline: "Meet Vivi. The AI money genius that fits in your pocket." Subhead: "The first AI Powered Account. The only way to Spend, Save, Earn & Travel with one app & One Card." The subhead restates the feature list without explaining what the AI actually does.

Why this matters for Vivi: "AI" in 2026 is a depreciated claim. Every fintech app — including the ones Vivi is competing with — says the same thing. Without a concrete "here's exactly what the AI does for you" proof point, the headline is category noise and the install CTA has nothing to push against.

COPY-03 Headline Specificity / COPY-08 Proof & Credibility

Recommendation

Replace the subhead with a specific AI capability: "Vivi watches your spending, moves your savings automatically, and finds the best FX rate before you travel — so you never think about money." Lead with a concrete outcome, not a feature list.

Effort: LowImpact: High
