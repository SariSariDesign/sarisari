Methodology

This audit evaluates basiccapital.com against 8 static UX dimensions (50+ criteria) using Sari Sari Design's UX Audit Reference Checklist, prioritized for a B2B SLG motion in retirement fintech.

Evaluation Lens

Basic Capital sells into CFOs and founders who already have a 401(k) provider. The audit prioritizes how clearly the site answers "why switch?" and whether novel features like the Retirement Mortgage are explained in a way that doesn't alienate compliance-cautious fiduciaries.

Why This Matters

For an early-stage retirement fintech, every demo request is expensive — paid search on 401(k) keywords is brutal. Marketing site friction lengthens payback periods and burns capital. A confusing hero or feature explanation that scares conservative CFOs is an avoidable tax on growth.

Top 5 Findings

Selected for business impact and relevance to Basic Capital's growth stage and SLG motion.

1
Content Hierarchy & CopywritingMajor

Hero headline sells a feature, not the reason to switch

"The 401(k) that gives you instant insights and data" describes a capability, not an outcome. A CFO evaluating whether to move off Guideline or Human Interest doesn't wake up wanting "instant insights" — they want lower fees, reduced liability, or a happier workforce.

Why this matters for Basic Capital: In a category where switching costs are high (plan transfers are administratively painful), the hero's job is to give a CFO a concrete reason the change is worth the pain. Feature-led copy doesn't clear that bar. Competitors win by leading with outcomes.

COPY-02 Benefit-led Framing / COPY-03 Headline Specificity

Recommendation

Rewrite the hero around the switch-reason. Options: "The 401(k) that eliminates compliance liability" or "The 401(k) that helps employees retire with 3x more savings." Lead with the outcome CFOs buy, not the feature that delivers it.

Effort: LowImpact: High
2
Content Hierarchy & CopywritingMajor

Retirement Mortgage is your differentiator but explained as an abstract ratio

The Retirement Mortgage — a novel leverage product for retirement savings — is the single most differentiated feature on the site. It's explained via an abstract $400/$100 visual that doesn't translate into a concrete employee outcome.

Why this matters for Basic Capital: Novel financial products need plain-language framing, especially with compliance-cautious fiduciaries. Abstract ratios without context can trigger "too good to be true" skepticism in exactly the buyers you need to win. A CFO's first reaction to anything they don't immediately understand is risk aversion.

COPY-07 Jargon & Readability / COPY-08 Proof & Credibility

Recommendation

Reframe around a concrete employee outcome: "Your employees retire with 3x more savings — here's the math." Include a named customer example, a compliance sign-off quote, and a 1-paragraph plain-language risk disclosure. Build trust before pitching the mechanism.

Effort: MediumImpact: High
3
Conversion Rate OptimizationMajor

Three audiences, one CTA path

The nav routes to "For Employers," "For Employees," and "For Advisors" — three very different intents — yet the CTA surface is the same across all three ("Talk to our team" / "See it in action").

Why this matters for Basic Capital: An employee landing here wants to check their balance, not book a sales call. An advisor wants a partner program. A CFO wants pricing and compliance confidence. Funneling all three into the same CTA path creates the wrong experience for two of three audiences and suppresses conversion on all of them.

CRO-04 Engagement Path Variety

Recommendation

Route each persona to a persona-specific landing page with a relevant CTA: Employers → "Book a demo" or "See pricing"; Employees → "Log in" or "Learn about my 401(k)"; Advisors → "Join the partner program."

Effort: MediumImpact: High
4
Conversion Rate OptimizationMajor

One testimonial is not enough for a retirement fintech

The site features a single named testimonial (Eli Wachs of Footprint) plus media quotes. For a company asking employers to move their employees' retirement assets, this is thin social proof.

Why this matters for Basic Capital: Retirement is among the highest-trust categories in fintech. Buyers transferring employee assets need overwhelming proof they're not making a mistake they'll have to explain to their team. One testimonial doesn't carry that weight — especially against established competitors with hundreds of published case studies.

CRO-07 Social Proof Depth

Recommendation

Add 4-6 more testimonials with specific outcomes ("switched from Guideline, saved 30% on fees, zero compliance escalations in 12 months"). Add customer logos. Add a "Trusted by [N] growing companies" counter. Publish 2-3 real case studies.

Effort: HighImpact: High
5
Visual & Brand DesignMinor

Information density reduces scannability for executive buyers

Several sections pack multiple feature claims into close visual proximity without dominant anchors. An executive buyer scanning for 10 seconds cannot tell which claim is the hero of each section.

Why this matters for Basic Capital: CFOs and founders scan; they don't read. Dense sections get skipped entirely. Every section that fails to land a single clear claim is a missed chance to build conviction before the demo request.

VD-02 Visual Hierarchy / COPY-06 Scannability

Recommendation

Pick one "hero moment" per section — one claim, one stat, or one image — and visually dominate it. Let supporting content be clearly secondary in size and weight.

Effort: MediumImpact: Medium
