## Notes for the Drafter

- Lead with results, not process. The reader wants to know "what happened" before "how you did it."
- Use real numbers. If you don't have exact metrics, ask the client or use directional language ("significantly reduced" vs. making up a percentage).
- Show the thinking, not just the screens. Anyone can show a pretty UI. The case study should demonstrate strategic design thinking.
- Keep it scannable. Use subheadings, bold key metrics, and keep paragraphs short.
