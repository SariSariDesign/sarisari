# Referral Request Template

Use this template when asking existing clients for referrals. The structure follows Alex Hormozi's $100M Leads framework: lead with value, make it easy, add scarcity.

---

## Structure

### 1. Open with Appreciation

Start by acknowledging the relationship and the work you've done together. Be genuine and specific about what went well.

### 2. The Ask (Make It Easy)

Ask for a specific type of referral, not a vague "know anyone who could use design help?" The more specific you are, the easier it is for them to think of someone.

**Example framing:** "Do you know any other fintech founders who are running into the same [specific problem you solved for them]? I have room for one more client this quarter and would love an intro if someone comes to mind."

### 3. Incentive (Preferred Rate, Not Discount)

Offer a tangible benefit for successful referrals. Frame it as a preferred rate or bonus, never as a discount.

**Example:** "As a thank you, I'd offer them our preferred-rate onboarding (saving $X on their first month) and extend the same to you on your next quarter."

### 4. Scarcity (Genuine, Not Manufactured)

Reference real capacity constraints. A two-person studio genuinely has limited bandwidth, so this is authentic, not a pressure tactic.

**Example:** "We can only take on one more retainer client this quarter, so I'm being selective about who we work with next."

### 5. Make the Next Step Effortless

Don't ask them to write an email or make a pitch on your behalf. Offer to do the work.

**Example:** "If anyone comes to mind, just shoot me their name and I'll reach out directly. No need to make a formal introduction."

---

## Notes for the Drafter

- Tone should match the client relationship. Casual for casual clients (like Lendzi CEO), more polished for formal relationships.
- Send this after a successful milestone or delivery, not during a rough patch.
- Follow up once if no response. Don't push beyond that.
- Track referral requests and outcomes in the Notion pipeline.
