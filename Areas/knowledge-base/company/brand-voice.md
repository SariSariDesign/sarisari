# Sari Sari Design — Brand Voice

## Voice Principles

**Confident but not arrogant.** We know our craft. We lead with expertise and back it up with work, not with buzzwords or inflated claims. We say "we've done this before" rather than "we're the best in the world."

**Direct and clear.** No fluff, no filler. Fintech founders are busy and skeptical. We respect their time by getting to the point. Short sentences. Concrete examples. Specific outcomes over vague promises.

**Warm and human.** We're a small studio, not a faceless agency. Our tone should feel like talking to a smart colleague, not reading a corporate brochure. First names, conversational phrasing, occasional humor when appropriate.

**Strategic, not just tactical.** We talk about business outcomes, not just design deliverables. "We'll improve your onboarding conversion" hits harder than "we'll redesign your onboarding screens."

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn outreach | Peer-to-peer, curious, non-pitchy | "Noticed you just raised your Series A — congrats. Curious how you're thinking about scaling design." |
| Proposals | Confident, diagnostic, specific | "Based on our audit, the biggest conversion leak is between KYC step 2 and step 3. Here's our plan." |
| Client comms | Casual, collaborative, proactive | "Quick heads up — we spotted a potential issue with the flow and have a fix ready. Want to walk through it?" |
| Website copy | Clear, benefit-led, concise | "Design that earns trust. We help fintech companies turn compliance into competitive advantage." |

## Words We Use

"Embedded partner" (not vendor, not contractor)
"Design system" (not style guide)
"High-trust flows" (not just UI/UX)
"Compliance UX" (specific, differentiating)
"Growth-stage" (not startup, not enterprise)
"Fractional Head of Design" (for Scale tier)

## Words We Avoid

"Unlimited" (signals commodity, not value)
"Pixel-perfect" (overused, says nothing)
"Full-service agency" (we're specialized, not generalist)
"Disrupting" / "revolutionizing" (empty tech clichés)
"Discount" (we say "preferred rate" when offering concessions)

## Cultural Note

"Sari Sari" comes from Filipino sari-sari stores, neighborhood shops that have a little bit of everything people need. The name reflects resourcefulness, community, and practical value. This cultural thread can show up authentically in personal brand content but should never be forced or tokenized in client-facing materials.
