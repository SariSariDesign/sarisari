# Client Onboarding Flow

## Overview

This document outlines the standard process for onboarding a new retainer client. The goal is to move from signed agreement to productive design work within the first week, while building the context depth that makes our embedded model work.

## Pre-Kickoff (Before Day 1)

1. **Contract and payment confirmed.** First month's retainer collected before work begins.
2. **Access granted.** Request access to: Figma workspace, product staging environment, analytics tools (if relevant), internal Slack or communication channel.
3. **Client brief completed.** Fill out the client brief template (`/clients/[client-name]/overview.md`) using information from discovery calls and any materials the client has shared.
4. **Internal kickoff.** Zach and Patricia align on client context, priorities, and the first two weeks of planned work.

## Week 1: Discovery and Foundation

- **Kickoff call** (60 min): Meet the key stakeholders. Align on goals, priorities, timeline, and communication cadence. Understand their definition of success for the first 90 days.
- **Product walkthrough:** Use the product as a real user would. Document friction points, UX issues, and compliance gaps.
- **Design audit:** Review existing design assets, brand guidelines, component libraries, and any prior design work. Assess the current state of their design system (or lack thereof).
- **Compliance review:** Identify which regulatory frameworks apply (SOC2, PCI DSS, KYC/AML) and how they currently manifest in the product UX.

## Week 2: First Deliverables

- Share initial audit findings and a prioritized list of design opportunities.
- Begin work on the highest-priority item identified in the kickoff.
- Establish the ongoing communication rhythm (weekly async updates, biweekly sync calls for Grow; more frequent for Scale).

## Ongoing

- Weekly async updates in the agreed channel (Slack, email, or Loom).
- Biweekly sync calls (Grow) or weekly (Scale).
- Monthly retrospective: What shipped, what's next, what's working, what isn't.
