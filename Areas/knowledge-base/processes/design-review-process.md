# Design Review Process

## Overview

This document describes how design work is reviewed and approved, both internally (Zach and Patricia) and with clients.

## Internal Review

Before any design work is shared with a client:

1. **Patricia creates the design** in Figma and marks it as ready for internal review.
2. **Zach reviews** for strategic alignment, brand consistency, and whether the work addresses the client's stated goals. Zach is not reviewing for pixel-level design decisions — that's Patricia's domain.
3. **Feedback is async first.** Comments go directly in Figma. If a sync conversation is needed, it happens within 24 hours.
4. **One round of internal revision** before client presentation. If it needs more than one round, that signals a misalignment in the brief, not a design quality issue.

## Client Review

1. **Present work in context**, not as isolated screens. Walk the client through the user flow, not a gallery of static frames. Loom videos or live walkthroughs are preferred over Figma links with no narration.
2. **Frame design decisions with rationale.** "We chose this layout because it reduces the number of steps in the KYC flow from 5 to 3" is better than "here's the new KYC screen."
3. **Collect structured feedback.** Ask specific questions rather than "what do you think?" Examples: "Does this flow match how your compliance team needs to verify identity?" or "Is the information hierarchy on this dashboard aligned with what your users check first?"
4. **Two rounds of client revision** are included in the standard engagement. Scope creep beyond two rounds should trigger a conversation about priorities, not silent overwork.
