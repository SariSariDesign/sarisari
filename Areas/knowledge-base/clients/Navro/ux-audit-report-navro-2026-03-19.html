Methodology

This audit evaluates Navro's marketing website across 11 UX dimensions using a structured reference checklist of 80+ criteria, supplemented by automated accessibility scanning, SEO metadata extraction, and visual inspection.

Evaluation Lens

Framed for a Series B, sales-led B2B fintech selling payments infrastructure to enterprise buyers — meaning the website must function as a credibility engine that converts high-intent visitors into demo requests while clearly routing multiple personas to relevant value propositions.

Why This Matters

For a Series B cross-border payments company in a crowded competitive landscape, website friction directly translates to lost pipeline. Enterprise buyers who can't quickly understand Navro's differentiation will bounce to a competitor before ever booking a demo. At this growth stage, every qualified demo request has outsized revenue impact.

Top 5 Findings

Selected for business impact, evidence quality, and relevance to Navro's growth stage and GTM model.

1
Conversion Rate Optimization Critical

Zero social proof on the homepage

The homepage contains no customer logos, testimonials, case study snippets, named quotes, or quantified results. The only external validation appears in the News section (press releases about partnerships and hiring), which is not positioned as social proof.

Why this matters for Navro: Enterprise B2B buyers rely heavily on social proof to validate purchasing decisions. Without evidence that real companies use and benefit from Navro, the website asks visitors to trust claims on faith alone. Competitors like Airwallex and Wise Platform prominently feature customer logos and quantified results above the fold. For a Series B company competing against $5B+ incumbents, social proof isn't a nice-to-have — it's the difference between a demo request and a bounce.

CRO-06 Trust Signal Placement    CRO-07 Social Proof Depth

Recommendation

Add a customer logo bar near the hero section (even 4-5 logos significantly increase credibility). Add 2-3 named testimonials with titles and company affiliations near CTAs. Include quantified results where possible. Place trust signals at decision points — near CTAs, after bold claims, and before the ask.

Effort: Medium Impact: High
2
CRO / Copywriting Critical

Hero section fails the 5-second test

The hero headline “The payments curation platform” is a category label, not a value proposition. It describes what Navro is, not what it does for the customer. There is no subheading explaining the customer benefit. Combined with a ~3 second animation delay before any content appears, a first-time visitor cannot answer three critical questions within 5 seconds: What does this product do? Who is it for? What should I do next?

Why this matters for Navro: The hero section is the single highest-impact real estate on any B2B website. For a sales-led model, failing the 5-second test means high-intent visitors from paid channels, referrals, or organic search cannot quickly confirm they're in the right place. "Payments curation" is a Navro-specific term — not an established category term that prospects search for. This directly impacts bounce rate and pipeline conversion.

CRO-01 Above-the-Fold Clarity    COPY-02 Benefit-Led Framing    COPY-03 Headline Specificity

Recommendation

Reframe the hero to lead with customer benefit: e.g., “Move Money to 200+ Territories Through One Connection” with “The payments curation platform” as a supporting descriptor. Add a benefit-led subheading that communicates the outcome. Ensure content renders instantly without animation dependency.

Effort: Low Impact: High
3
Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA) Critical

All 33 images have empty alt text

Every image on the homepage (33 total) has alt="", marking them all as decorative. This includes solution card images, the hero visual, the Chaos vs. Curation product diagram, news thumbnails, and the company logo. None of these are purely decorative — they all convey meaningful information.

Why this matters for Navro: This violates WCAG 2.1 AA (a legal compliance standard in the UK, EU, and US) and eliminates all image SEO value. Screen reader users — including potential customers and partners — receive no information about any visual content. The Chaos vs. Curation diagram, Navro's strongest visual asset, is completely invisible to assistive technology. Additionally, enterprise procurement teams increasingly evaluate accessibility compliance as part of vendor assessment.

ACC-01 Non-text Content (WCAG 1.1.1)    SEO-05 Image Alt Text

Recommendation

Audit every image and add descriptive alt text to all informational images. Example for the comparison diagram: “Diagram comparing fragmented multi-provider payment setup versus unified single-connection setup with Navro.” Keep alt="" only for truly decorative elements (background patterns, spacers).

Effort: Low Impact: High
4
Visual & Brand Design Critical

Hero renders as a blank dark screen for ~3 seconds

The initial visual impression upon landing on navro.com is a dark navy/black screen with only the navigation bar visible. The hero content — headline, stats, CTAs, and product visuals — are all hidden behind a JavaScript animation that takes approximately 3 seconds to execute. Additionally, when the content does appear, the headline text is clipped on the left edge of the viewport.

Why this matters for Navro: Enterprise fintech buyers form trust judgments within milliseconds. A blank screen signals potential technical issues and fails to communicate professionalism. For a payments infrastructure provider — a category where reliability is the core value proposition — a visually broken first impression is particularly damaging. Competitors show fully rendered, polished heroes on first paint.

VD-01 First Impression and Credibility    N-01 Visibility of System Status

Recommendation

Render hero content server-side or via CSS-first approach so it's visible on first contentful paint. Animation can enhance the experience but should never gate it. Fix the viewport clipping bug (likely a CSS overflow or transform issue) to ensure all content is fully visible at standard breakpoints.

Effort: Medium Impact: High
5
Conversion Rate Optimization Major

No engagement path variety for different intent levels

The site offers only two conversion actions: “Schedule a Call” (high intent) and “Learn More” (links to product pages). There are no mid-funnel engagement paths: no product tours, interactive demos, case study downloads, whitepapers, ROI calculators, or newsletter signups.

Why this matters for Navro: Not all visitors arrive ready to speak with sales. Mid-funnel visitors — those researching, comparing, or building a business case internally — have no way to engage without committing to a sales conversation. This means Navro captures only high-intent visitors and loses the larger pool of mid-intent traffic that could be nurtured into pipeline. Given Navro's relatively low brand awareness compared to Airwallex or Payoneer, many visitors will be in research mode.

CRO-04 Engagement Path Variety

Recommendation

Add at least one mid-funnel engagement path (product tour, downloadable guide, or interactive demo). Consider a secondary hero CTA that's lower commitment than “Schedule a Call” — e.g., “See How It Works.” Add a newsletter or content signup in the footer. This creates a nurture path for visitors who aren't yet ready for sales.

Effort: Medium Impact: High
